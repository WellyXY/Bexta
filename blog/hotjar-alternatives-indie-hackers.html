Hotjar Alternatives for Indie Hackers: Tools That Don't Break the Budget

By Racoonn Team · 2026-03-27 · 6 min read

Why Indie Hackers Need Hotjar Alternatives

Hotjar's Business plan starts at $99/month and scales based on sessions. For an indie hacker with under 10,000 monthly sessions, this is a significant chunk of revenue. The Plus plan at $39/month is more accessible, but even this requires a product that generates consistent revenue to justify. For pre-revenue or early-stage projects, there are better options.

The good news: Hotjar's core value — understanding how users interact with your pages — can be replicated with free and cheap alternatives that have improved significantly in recent years. The trade-off is usually in data volume, advanced features, or support quality.

Microsoft Clarity: Free and Surprisingly Good

Microsoft Clarity is entirely free with no session limits. It provides session recordings, heatmaps, and basic behavior analytics. For indie hackers who need to understand where users click, scroll, and drop off, Clarity covers 90% of Hotjar's functionality at zero cost.

Clarity's session recordings are particularly good — they capture rage clicks, dead clicks, and JavaScript errors automatically. The interface is clean and the data is accessible within minutes of adding the script. The main limitation is analytics depth: Clarity doesn't segment sessions by custom properties or provide funnel analysis.

Plausible and PostHog: Privacy-Friendly Alternatives

Plausible Analytics offers lightweight, privacy-focused analytics at $9/month for up to 10,000 pageviews. It doesn't have session recordings, but its traffic analytics are clean and GDPR-compliant — an important consideration for European makers. For many indie products, understanding traffic sources and page performance is more actionable than watching session recordings.

PostHog is open-source and offers a generous free tier (1 million events/month). It includes session recordings, feature flags, A/B testing, and product analytics. The setup is more complex than Clarity, but for technical founders who want to self-host and have full data ownership, PostHog is unmatched at any price.

Choosing the Right Tool for Your Stage

Pre-revenue or early-stage: Start with Microsoft Clarity (free) and Google Analytics 4 (free). This covers traffic analytics and session recordings. Under $10k MRR: Add Plausible or a self-hosted PostHog for better privacy compliance and event tracking. When conversions matter: Use Racoonn to understand specifically why your landing page is or isn't converting — the investment pays back quickly if it identifies one fixable issue.

Don't use tools you won't regularly check. A $0 tool you review weekly beats a $100 tool you never open. Pick one analytics tool, one session recording tool, and review both at least monthly.
