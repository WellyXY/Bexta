50 Landing Page Headline Examples You Can Steal

Your headline is the first — and often only — thing visitors read. If it doesn't hook them, nothing else matters. Here are 50 proven landing page headline formulas with real examples.

Outcome-First Headlines

These work because they lead with what the user GETS, not what you built. Examples: "Double Your Trial Signups in 30 Days", "Cut Your Customer Churn in Half", "Stop Losing Customers to Bad Onboarding", "Turn Visitors Into Paying Customers", "Get Your First 100 Users Without Paid Ads"

Problem-Aware Headlines

These work when visitors know they have a problem but don't know the solution yet. Examples: "Why Are Your Visitors Leaving Without Signing Up?", "Your Landing Page Converts 2% — It Should Convert 8%", "Tired of Guessing Why Users Drop Off?", "Most Free Trials Never Become Paid Users. Here's Why Yours Will."

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a landing page headline be?

6-12 words is the sweet spot. Long enough to communicate the core value, short enough to read at a glance. Avoid headlines that need a second read to understand.

Should my landing page headline include keywords?

If the page gets organic traffic, yes — but don't stuff keywords unnaturally. A good headline reads well for humans first, search engines second.

How do I know if my headline is working?

Track scroll depth. If most visitors don't scroll past the hero, your headline isn't compelling enough to make them want to know more.
