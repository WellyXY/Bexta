How to Build a Webinar Registration Page That Fills Seats

Webinar registration pages have one job: convince the right people to sign up. Most fail because they list topics instead of outcomes, or bury the value below the fold.

The 5 Elements Every Registration Page Needs

(1) Outcome-first headline: what will attendees be able to DO after? (2) Speaker credibility: photo, title, one-line bio. (3) Agenda bullets: 3-5 specific takeaways. (4) Date/time with timezone. (5) Simple form: first name + email is enough.

Registration Rate Benchmarks

A good webinar registration page converts 30-50% of visitors who arrive via targeted traffic (email list, LinkedIn ads). Cold traffic (display ads) is typically 15-25%. If you're below these, check your headline and form placement.

Test Your Registration Page Before the Webinar

Send the page to 5 people in your target audience before promoting it. Ask: "What do you think this webinar is about? Would you sign up?" Their answers reveal whether your copy is clear.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a webinar registration page be?

Short. The form should be visible without scrolling on desktop. Use a headline, 3-5 bullet outcomes, speaker bio, and registration form. No more.

What is a good webinar registration conversion rate?

30-50% for warm traffic (your email list), 15-25% for paid cold traffic.

Should I require a phone number on my webinar registration form?

No. Adding phone number fields drops conversion significantly. Stick to first name + email unless you have a compelling reason.
