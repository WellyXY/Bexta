Color Psychology for Landing Pages: What Actually Affects Conversion

Does button color really affect conversion rates? Yes — but not in the way most articles suggest. The secret isn't picking the "best" color. It's picking the right color for your context.

The CTA Button Color Myth

You've heard "red buttons convert better" or "green means go." These rules are oversimplified. The real principle: your CTA button should be the highest contrast element on the page. If your design is mostly blue, an orange button stands out. If it's already orange, another orange button disappears.

Color and Trust

Blue dominates SaaS and finance because research shows it's associated with reliability and trust. Red can signal urgency but also alarm. Green works for anything associated with growth or money. For B2B, avoid harsh colors that feel aggressive — they can undermine trust before the user even reads your copy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What color CTA button converts best?

The one with the most contrast against your background. There is no universally best color. Orange and green tend to work well on blue/white pages, but test your specific design.

Does background color affect bounce rate?

Yes. Busy or dark backgrounds increase cognitive load and tend to increase bounce rate. Clean white or light grey backgrounds keep focus on content and convert better for most SaaS products.

Should I use multiple colors on a landing page?

Limit to 2-3 colors: a primary brand color, a high-contrast CTA color, and a neutral background. More colors create visual noise and reduce focus on the conversion action.
