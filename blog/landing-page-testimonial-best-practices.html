Landing Page Testimonials: How to Use Them Right

Testimonials are the highest-ROI trust signal on a landing page — but only when done right. Generic, vague testimonials actually hurt credibility. Here's how to use testimonials that actually move conversion rates.

What Makes a Testimonial Convert

High-converting testimonials are: specific (mention concrete results — "Increased our trial conversion from 12% to 31%"), from real people with full name and photo, relevant to the potential buyer's situation ("As a founder with no design team..."), and address a specific objection ("I was worried it would take weeks to set up — it took 2 hours").

Where to Place Testimonials on Your Page

Near every CTA: a testimonial immediately above or below your call-to-action button directly increases click rate. In the hero section: one short quote from a recognizable customer name builds immediate trust. In a dedicated social proof section: a grid of 3-6 testimonials mid-page. Near the pricing section: testimonials about value/ROI address the biggest objection at the point of decision.

How to Get Better Testimonials

Most founders make the mistake of asking "Can you write a testimonial?" Instead, ask: "What specific result did you achieve using [product]?" and "What would you tell a skeptical friend about it?" Then turn their answers into a structured quote with their approval. Better questions → better testimonials.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many testimonials should a landing page have?

For a typical landing page: 3-6 testimonials distributed throughout the page. More than 8-10 starts to feel overwhelming. Prioritize quality over quantity — one powerful specific testimonial beats five vague ones.

Can I use anonymous testimonials?

Anonymous testimonials ("— Marketing Manager at Fortune 500") are better than nothing but convert worse than named testimonials. Always get permission to use full name and company. Add a photo to increase trust further.

Should landing page testimonials be video or text?

Both have value. Text testimonials are faster to consume and easier to skim. Video testimonials build more emotional trust but require more attention. Consider text testimonials throughout the page with one featured video testimonial near the top.
